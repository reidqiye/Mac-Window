Assumption challenged: Difficulty should escalate over time.

In the original Pong design, the game assumes that increasing difficulty creates excitement. This assumption is reinforced in the tutorial by accelerating the ball every time it is successfully hit by a paddle. I challenged this assumption by reversing the rule: instead of speeding up, the ball gradually slows down after each successful hit.

This single change significantly altered the gameplay experience. Skilled players were no longer rewarded with intensity or urgency. Instead, precise play resulted in longer rallies and a slower, more suspended game state. Rather than pushing the match toward a climax, successful play began to delay resolution.

Through this change, the game shifted from a test of reaction speed to an exercise in endurance and restraint. It exposed how difficulty escalation is not a neutral design choice, but a value system embedded in the rules. By slowing the game down as players performed well, the game questioned the assumption that progress must always be tied to increasing pressure or challenge.
